Scenario

You are in charge of managing exchange risk for Generic Company, a United States based firm who specializes in manufacturing widgets. These widgets require a rare metal which is mined in Germany. Therefore, the company imports the rare metal from Europe to be used in production. The production of the firm resides in Mexico, and thus the firm pays all production costs in Pesos. Finally, the product is then exported from Mexico and imported into the United States to be sold to its final consumers. Each Round consists of 1 year, and the financial charts will update with the past years information added on.



Since Generic Company is tied with international affairs, the company faces many risks related to exchange rate risks.

  • Transaction Risk - A company has a contract for goods in a foreign currency and the exchange rate changes unfavorably.
    • The import of rare metals from Germany has a risk with the Dollar to Euro exchange rate.
  • Translation Risk - A company with assets, liabilities, and income in a foreign currency will be affected by a change in the exchange rate.
    • Generic Company is a U.S. based company and should be valued based on value in U.S. Dollars.
  • Economic/Operating Exposure Risk - A corporation that competes against or outsources to a foreign company will be impacted if the foreign currency changes.
    • Generic Company outsources work to Mexico, and thus must pay in Pesos for production costs.


Generic Company has three accounts, one for each currency it trades in which is $, €, and ₱. You will be able to invest these accounts at the interest rate in those specific markets. Then every Round you will be asked to make a decision on how to handle exchange rate risk based on a future expense in the future.
Each round you will have the choice on how to manage the exchange rate risk based on financial information.

  • Purchase/Sell Forward Contracts - Lock in a rate for a currency exchange now and trade in the future.
  • Purchase Calls - A currency option that has a payoff when the underlying asset is worth more than the strike price at expiration.
  • Purchase Puts - A currency option that has a payoff when the underlying asset is worth less than the strike price at expiration.
These can be found under decisions and the information to base your decisions on can be found in Financial Information. If you do not purchase a forward contract or any options, then all currency is bought at market rate in the year.


Production: Each round you will decide how much to produce and the simulation will reflect proper costs. Price sensitivity of the product matters linearly in the market, and thus it is important to set manufacturing levels based on the price you think you can get based on exchange rates. Price is adjusted for a 30% contribution margin. Based on this input, basic values will be pre-populated to meet this minimum demand for the user to see.

Loans: Players have the ability to take out and repay loans in U.S. Dollars. Loans are priced at 2% premium to the U.S. Dollar interest rate. Loans are also taken out if the player lacks sufficient funds and is then converted into the currency to cover funds for production or material costs. At the end of the simulation loans will be substracted from your final U.S. Dollars standing.

Interest Rates: All funds gain interest for when you hold those funds during the round based on that currency's interest rate.

Translation: At the end of the simulation all the assets the company has will be converted into U.S. dollars based on the current exchange rate.
